i693 USCIS Medical Exam

Dr. Efraim Kessous — USCIS Authorized Civil Surgeon

If you're applying for a green card, USCIS requires a medical examination and Form I-693 completed by an authorized civil surgeon. The exam screens for communicable diseases and verifies your vaccinations per CDC technical instructions.

Before Your Visit

What to Bring

Come prepared with the following to ensure a smooth appointment and timely I-693 form processing.

After Your Exam

Turnaround Time

If all test results come back normal, expect a call within 1–2 weeks to collect your signed and sealed I-693 paperwork. Submit the sealed envelope to USCIS with your Form I-485 application.

FAA Physical Examinations

Dr. Efraim Kessous — Senior Aviation Medical Examiner (AME)

The Federal Aviation Administration requires all pilots to pass a medical examination with an authorized Aviation Medical Examiner (AME). The exam evaluates your physical and mental fitness to operate an aircraft.

Certificate Classes

Who Needs an FAA Physical

All pilots need a valid FAA medical certificate. The class depends on how you fly.

1st Class — Airline Transport 2nd Class — Commercial 3rd Class — Student & Recreational
Before Your Visit

How to Prepare

Complete your FAA MedXPress form before your appointment. Expect hearing, vision, and urine tests, plus an ECG for first-class certificates.

AME Guide AMAS Medication Database
Renewals

How Often to Renew

Frequency depends on your certificate class and age — ranging from every 6 months to every 60 months. See the FAA medical certification FAQ for details.

6–60 Month Intervals Age-Dependent
